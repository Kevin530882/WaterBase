# WBSI Severity Distribution Chart - User Guide

## 🌊 What is WBSI?

The **WaterBase Severity Index (WBSI)** is an advanced pollution assessment system that analyzes community reports to provide reliable pollution severity scores for water bodies. Unlike simple averages, WBSI focuses on community consensus while remaining robust to outliers.

## 📊 How to Use the Chart

### Accessing the Chart
1. **Open the Map View** in your WaterBase application
2. **Select any report** from the report list or click a marker on the map
3. **The pollution analysis chart will appear** below the Type/Status filters in the sidebar
4. **Click the expand button** (chevron) to open the full tabbed interface

### Chart Layout

The chart has a **collapsible design** to preserve sidebar space:
- **Collapsed**: Shows only WBSI score, report count, and consensus percentage
- **Expanded**: Reveals four comprehensive tabs with detailed analysis

## 🗂️ Four Analysis Tabs

### 1. 📋 Overview Tab
**Quick summary of the pollution assessment**

- **WBSI Score**: The calculated pollution severity percentage
- **Assessment Level**: Clean, Moderate, High, or Critical pollution
- **Key Statistics**: Peak severity, community agreement, total reports, outliers
- **Action Required**: Recommended response based on pollution level
- **Warnings**: Alerts for split community opinions or data reliability issues

### 2. 📈 Distribution Tab (Chart)
**Visual representation of pollution severity patterns**

- **Interactive Chart**: Combined bar chart and trend line showing report distribution
- **Color-coded Bars**: Green (low), Yellow (medium), Orange (high), Red (critical)
- **Trend Line**: Smooth curve showing overall pollution pattern
- **Peak Indicator**: Dashed line showing most commonly reported severity
- **Consensus Zone**: Shaded area where most reports agree
- **Chart Guide**: Instructions on how to read each element
- **Pollution Breakdown**: Percentage distribution across severity levels

### 3. 📄 Summary Tab
**Executive-level analysis and recommendations**

- **Executive Summary**: Comprehensive assessment in plain language
- **Reliability Assessment**: How confident you can be in the results
- **Recommended Actions**: Specific next steps based on pollution level
- **Next Steps List**: Detailed action items prioritized by importance
- **Key Insights**: Important observations about the data pattern

### 4. 💡 Insights Tab (Guide)
**Educational content and scenario matching**

- **Chart Interpretation Guide**: How to understand different visual patterns
- **Similar Situations**: Real-world scenarios matching your data
- **Scenario Examples**: Different pollution patterns and their meanings
- **Pro Tips**: Expert guidance on interpreting results
- **Action Recommendations**: Context-specific advice for each scenario type

## 🎯 Understanding Your Results

### WBSI Score Ranges
- **0-25%**: Clean Water - Minimal pollution concerns
- **25-50%**: Moderate Pollution - Regular monitoring recommended  
- **50-75%**: High Pollution - Immediate action required
- **75-100%**: Critical Pollution - Emergency response needed

### Consensus Percentage
- **>80%**: High reliability - Strong community agreement
- **60-80%**: Good reliability - Reasonable consensus
- **40-60%**: Moderate reliability - Some disagreement exists
- **<40%**: Low reliability - Significant disagreement, investigate further

### Report Count Impact
- **<3 reports**: Very limited data, results may not be representative
- **3-5 reports**: Small sample, consider gathering more data
- **5-10 reports**: Reasonable sample size for initial assessment
- **>10 reports**: Good statistical confidence in results

## 📍 Location-Based Analysis

The system automatically:
- **Finds nearby reports** within 5km of your selected location
- **Combines multiple reports** for comprehensive area assessment
- **Shows location name** in the chart header for context
- **Calculates consensus** across all relevant reports in the area

## ⚠️ Special Indicators

### Split Consensus Warning
When community opinions are divided between two distinct pollution levels:
- **Yellow warning box** appears in Overview tab
- **Multiple peaks** visible in Distribution chart
- **Investigation recommended** to understand disagreement

### Outlier Detection
Reports that are statistically unusual compared to the majority:
- **Automatically identified** using statistical methods
- **Listed in Summary tab** for investigation
- **May indicate**: Special conditions, measurement errors, or localized effects

## 🔄 Interpreting Different Chart Patterns

### Single Peak Pattern
- **Meaning**: Strong community agreement on pollution level
- **Action**: High confidence in results, proceed with recommended actions

### Multiple Peaks Pattern  
- **Meaning**: Community divided between different assessments
- **Action**: Investigate reasons for disagreement before major decisions

### Flat Distribution Pattern
- **Meaning**: Wide variation in reported pollution levels
- **Action**: Gather more data or investigate measurement methods

### Outlier Pattern
- **Meaning**: Most reports agree, but some are very different
- **Action**: Focus on consensus while investigating unusual reports

## 🛠️ Technical Features

### Advanced Mathematical Foundation
- **Kernel Density Estimation** for smooth distribution curves
- **Modal analysis** to find pollution consensus peaks
- **Weighted calculations** considering report quality and recency
- **Statistical outlier detection** using standard deviation analysis
- **Small-sample correction** to prevent overconfidence with limited data

### User-Friendly Design
- **Collapsible interface** preserves screen space
- **Tabbed organization** prevents information overload  
- **Responsive design** works on all device sizes
- **Interactive tooltips** provide detailed explanations
- **Color-coded indicators** make results instantly recognizable
